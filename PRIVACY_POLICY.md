# Privacy Policy for TabDeep Chrome Extension

*Last updated: March 21, 2026*

## Overview

TabDeep is a Chrome extension that provides detailed insights into your browser history across all your synced devices. We take your privacy seriously and are committed to protecting your personal information.

## Data Collection and Usage

### What Data We Access

**Local Browser Data:**
- Your browsing history (URLs, page titles, visit times)
- Currently open tabs across all Chrome-synced devices
- Chrome session information for device identification
- Visit transition types (how you accessed each page)
- Website favicons for visual identification

**Chrome Sync Data:**
- Device names and sync status
- Tab sessions from other Chrome-synced devices
- Sync timestamps for cross-device correlation

### How We Use Your Data

**Data Processing:**
- All data analysis occurs **locally** within your browser
- No data is transmitted to external servers or third parties
- History correlation and filtering happen on your device only
- Theme preferences are stored in Chrome's sync storage

**Display Purposes:**
- Show organized, searchable history across devices
- Display favicon images for visual page identification
- Provide visit method analysis (clicked links, typed URLs, etc.)
- Present sync status and device information

### What We Do NOT Do

**We Do NOT:**
- Send your browsing data to external servers
- Store your data on our servers or cloud services
- Share your information with third parties
- Track your browsing behavior outside the extension
- Collect personal identifying information
- Use analytics or telemetry services
- Monetize your browsing data

## Data Storage and Security

**Local Storage Only:**
- All processing happens within Chrome's secure extension environment
- Only theme preferences are synced via Chrome's built-in sync service
- No databases or external storage systems are used
- Data remains under your control through Chrome's native settings

**Security Measures:**
- Extension uses Chrome's secure API framework
- Input sanitization prevents code injection attacks
- Proper error handling protects against data corruption
- No external network requests for data processing

## Permissions Explained

**Why We Need Each Permission:**

- **`history`**: To access and display your browsing history
- **`sessions`**: To discover and sync data from your other devices
- **`tabs`**: To monitor currently open tabs and their status
- **`favicon`**: To display website icons for visual identification
- **`storage`**: To remember your theme preference (dark/light mode)

**Chrome Security:**
- All permissions are enforced by Chrome's extension security model
- You can revoke permissions at any time through Chrome settings
- Extension operates within Chrome's sandboxed environment

## Your Privacy Rights

### Data Control

**You Have Full Control:**
- Disable the extension at any time through Chrome settings
- Clear your browsing history using Chrome's built-in tools
- Manage Chrome sync settings independently
- Uninstall the extension to stop all data access

**Chrome Sync Control:**
- Your Chrome sync settings control data availability
- Disabling sync will limit cross-device functionality
- You can manage sync per-device through Chrome settings

### Data Retention

**Local Processing:**
- No data retention by the extension itself
- Data availability follows Chrome's native retention policies
- History visibility controlled by Chrome's privacy settings

## Third-Party Services

**No Third-Party Data Sharing:**
- Extension operates independently without external services
- No analytics, crash reporting, or telemetry services
- No advertising networks or tracking pixels
- Favicon requests go directly to Chrome's internal API

## Changes to Privacy Policy

**Policy Updates:**
- Users will be notified of significant privacy policy changes
- Updates will be communicated through extension updates
- Continued use implies acceptance of updated terms
- Previous versions available upon request
